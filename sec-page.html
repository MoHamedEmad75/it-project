Most Knowed Cups:

World Cup

World cup is the most important football competition held under the auspices of the Fédération Internationale de Football Association (FIFA). The World Cup has been held every four years since 1930, with the exception of the 1942 and 1946 tournaments, which were canceled due to World War II. The current format of the tournament, since 1998, features 32 national teams divided into eight groups, competing for the title for a full month in the host country's stadiums. These teams qualify for the tournament through a qualifying system held over three years.

The Brazilian national team is the most crowned with the cup, having won it five times in the years: 1958, 1962, 1970, 1994 and 2002.

UEFA Champions League

UEFA Champions League is the most important club football tournament in the world.Prior to 1992, the tournament was officially called the European Champion Clubs' Cup, but is commonly referred to as the European Cup or the European Champions' Cup. Initially, it was a knockout tournament involving only the league champions of each country, along with the defending champions, who would then participate in the next edition to defend their title. The tournament began to expand in 1990, with the integration of a home-and-away group stage and an increase in the number of teams. In 1993, it was renamed the UEFA Champions League, and the number of teams increased to 32. The new Champions League began in the 1992–93 season.

The title has been won by 23 different clubs, 13 of which have won it more than once. Real Madrid is the club with the most wins, having won the tournament 15 times.

CAF Champions League

The CAF Champions League is an annual African football championship organized by the Confederation of African Football (CAF) since 1964 for the best African football clubs. It is the premier club football competition on the African continent.Approximately 58 teams are participating in the tournament, which includes the clubs that have won the league title in the African leagues, in addition to the runner-up of the general league from the top 12 clubs in the African club rankings.

Al Ahly is the most successful club in the competition's history, having won the tournament 12 times, followed by Zamalek and TP Mazembe, each with five titles. Egyptian clubs have won the most titles, with 18.

Africa Cup of Nations

The Africa Cup of Nations (French: Coupe d'Afrique des Nations), commonly known as the CAN or TotalEnergies Africa Cup of Nations, is the premier international men's football competition in Africa. Sanctioned by the Confederation of African Football (CAF), it was first held in 1957 in Khartoum, Sudan. Since 1968, it has been held every two years.The final tournament has had 16 participants since 1998 (16 teams were scheduled to compete in 1996, but Nigeria withdrew, reducing the number of teams to 15, a move repeated in 2010 with Togo). Until 2017, the format remained unchanged, with 16 teams drawn into four groups of four, with the top two teams from each group advancing to the knockout stage. On 20 July 2017, the Africa Cup of Nations was moved from January to June and expanded to 24 teams from 16.

Egypt is the most successful nation in the tournament's history, having won the tournament seven times (including when Egypt was known as the United Arab Republic between 1958 and 1961). Three different trophies have been awarded in the tournament's history, with Ghana and Cameroon winning the first two trophies after each winning the tournament three times. The current trophy was first awarded in 2002 and has been held indefinitely by Egypt after winning their third consecutive title in 2010.
